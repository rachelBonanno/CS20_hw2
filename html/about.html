About.

STEM events are hard to find and even if you know what you are looking for you are probably still missing out on amazing opportunities all around you. So why not make a place for people to find events, clubs, activities, organizations, and other resources?

Established in 2020

“During my high school junior year (2020) I participated in Girls With Impact and brought this idea to life. Through Girls With Impact, I was able to take my idea and break it down into what I needed to do and how I could do it. I never thought at 17 that I would be starting my own business. And I definitely never anticipated bringing it to life during a pandemic. As my website grew so did my ideas. I wanted it to be easy to use with a lot of opportunities for ways to interact with STEM. The problem was I only live in one place. Therefore, the opportunities I know about are only in my area and a few other states. I know that not everyone who is interested in STEM lives in the Northeast and I did not want to limit opportunities to that area. This is where the idea of being able to contact I-STEM with opportunities in one’s region, town, city, or village came into play. I ]realized I can't be everywhere at one time but if we band together and share opportunities the task of having events across the country does not seem as hard anymore. As said by the Salvation Army, sharing is caring. This website is a resource I wish I had when I started getting interested in STEM. I can only imagine what I would be doing now if I had all of these opportunities at my fingertips from the start. I am thrilled that I was able to bring my idea to life and share my passion for STEM with the world.”

- Said by the Founder -

Our Goal

To inspire the underrepresented groups in STEM, through sharing events, that one may have not found out about otherwise, in order to close the gap and equal the field.

Our Team

Rachel Bonanno

Founder

I have always been a STEM kid. I love math and science and I always knew I wanted to spend the rest of my life doing something in one of those fields. I really wanted to go to space, I still do. My dream was to be the first child in space and the first person on Mars. It wasn't until 6th grade though that I learned the true meaning of STEM. Who knew I could do more than the 5 core classes. When I was introduced to robotics and coding I just fell in love. I wanted more. I did not want to stop learning. When I started to develop and show the skills I had in the technology my mother started to research ways for me to grow and learn more. Over time it became clear that finding STEM events was not the easiest thing. You have to know exactly what you are looking for and even if you do, you are probably still missing out on amazing opportunities all around you. So why not make a place for people to find events, clubs, activities, organizations, and other resources?
